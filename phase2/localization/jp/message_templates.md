## Cultural Notes for Japan Outreach

### Do's:
- Always use 敬語 (keigo - honorific language)
- Include proper 挨拶 (aisatsu - greetings)
- Express gratitude frequently
- Be humble and indirect
- Use 「〜させていただく」(humble form)
- Follow up promptly but patiently

### Don'ts:
- Don't be pushy or aggressive
- Don't skip formalities
- Don't expect immediate responses
- Don't use casual language
- Don't make demands
- Don't ignore hierarchy

### Response Handling:
- **No response after 1 week**: Send polite follow-up
- **Declined meeting**: Thank them and keep door open
- **Interested**: Move slowly, build relationship
- **Needs more info**: Provide detailed documentation

### Meeting Etiquette:
1. Arrive 10 minutes early
2. Exchange business cards formally
3. Wait for host to indicate seating
4. Present materials in Japanese
5. Allow time for questions
6. Avoid hard deadlines
7. Send thank you email within 24 hours
