## Cultural Notes for Singapore Outreach

### Do's:
- ✅ **Be punctual** - Singapore values time highly
- ✅ **Be direct and clear** - Efficient communication preferred
- ✅ **Focus on data** - Evidence-based decision making
- ✅ **Show compliance readiness** - Regulations are important
- ✅ **Respect multiculturalism** - Diverse society
- ✅ **Follow proper channels** - Government procedures matter
- ✅ **Be professional** - High standards expected
- ✅ **Demonstrate global perspective** - Singapore is international

### Don'ts:
- ❌ Don't be late - It's considered very disrespectful
- ❌ Don't waste time with unnecessary information
- ❌ Don't ignore regulations or compliance
- ❌ Don't be culturally insensitive
- ❌ Don't bypass official processes
- ❌ Don't over-promise without evidence
- ❌ Don't assume one-size-fits-all for ethnic groups

### Business Etiquette:

**Greetings:**
- Handshake is standard
- Business card exchange (not as formal as Japan)
- Use proper titles (Mr., Ms., Dr.)

**Meetings:**
- Start on time, end on time
- Agenda-driven and focused
- Data and evidence are key
- Questions are encouraged
- Decisions are made carefully but not slowly

**Communication Style:**
- Direct but polite
- English is primary business language
- Concise emails preferred
- Bullet points valued
- Clear action items

**Dress Code:**
- Business professional
- Smart casual acceptable in tech sector
- Conservative and neat

### Government Engagement:

**Key Points:**
1. **Follow official channels** - Don't try shortcuts
2. **Prepare thoroughly** - Documentation must be complete
3. **Show economic benefit** - Job creation, GDP contribution
4. **Demonstrate compliance** - Regulatory adherence is critical
5. **Be patient but persistent** - Processes take time
6. **Highlight Singapore benefits** - Why Singapore as hub

**Required Documentation:**
- Business registration/incorporation plans
- Technical whitepaper
- Regulatory compliance assessment
- Economic impact study
- Team credentials
- Financial projections
- Timeline and milestones

### Networking Opportunities:

**Key Events:**
- Singapore FinTech Festival (November)
- MAS conferences and briefings
- IMDA industry forums
- Singapore Week of Innovation & Technology (SWITCH)
- Blockchain Association Singapore meetups

**Networking Venues:**
- Marina Bay Sands area (business district)
- Raffles Place
- One-North tech hub
- Co-working spaces (WeWork, JustCo)

### Response Handling:

**Positive Interest:**
- Schedule meeting within 1 week
- Prepare comprehensive documentation
- Have technical team available
- Show compliance readiness

**Needs More Information:**
- Provide detailed documentation immediately
- Offer technical briefing
- Share case studies and references
- Connect with existing Singapore partners

**No Response After 2 Weeks:**
- One polite follow-up email
- Try alternative contact if available
- Attend relevant industry events
- Consider Singapore FinTech Association introduction

### Best Contact Times:
- **Business Hours**: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM SGT (Singapore Time)
- **Avoid**: Public holidays, weekends
- **Lunch**: 12:00-2:00 PM (many have lunch meetings)
- **Response Expectation**: Same business day or next

### Grant Application Tips:

**Enterprise Development Grant (EDG):**
- Up to 50% funding for qualifying projects
- Focus on innovation and capability building
- Singapore company required
- Strong business case needed

**Startup SG Founder:**
- S$30,000 grant for first-time entrepreneurs
- Mentorship included
- Must be Singapore citizen/PR

**MAS FinTech Sandbox:**
- Test innovations in controlled environment
- Regulatory guidance provided
- Clear exit criteria

### Language & Communication:

**Official Languages:** English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil
**Business Language:** English is standard
**Email Style:** Professional, concise, clear
**Formality Level:** Professional but not overly formal

**Useful Phrases:**
- "Thank you" - Standard
- Keep it professional and clear
- Singlish in very casual contexts only (not business)

### Success Metrics:
- Government meeting success rate: >60%
- MoU signing rate: >40%
- Sandbox approval: 25-30%
- Time to first partnership: 2-3 months
- Grant approval rate: 30-40% (with good preparation)
