## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1: Final Preparations
**Tasks:**
1. Complete all bilingual materials
2. Engage Japanese cultural advisor
3. Set up Japanese email/phone capabilities
4. Research specific Sony contacts via LinkedIn, industry events
5. Identify mutual connections for warm introductions

**Deliverables:**
- Finalized pitch deck in両言語 (both languages)
- Contact list with 10+ potential entry points
- Cultural briefing document for team
- Japanese business card designs (名刺)

### Week 2: Initial Outreach
**Tasks:**
1. Send formal introduction emails (use templates from localization/jp/)
2. Connect on LinkedIn with appropriate formality
3. Reach out through any mutual connections
4. Follow up with physical mail if appropriate (still valued in Japan)

**Best Practices:**
- Use 敬語 (keigo - honorific language) consistently
- Send emails Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM-4 PM JST
- Include both Japanese and English versions
- Be patient - response may take 1-2 weeks

### Week 3: Relationship Building
**Tasks:**
1. Polite follow-up to initial outreach (if no response)
2. Engage with Sony's public content (conferences, press releases)
3. Attend Japan-focused tech/entertainment events (virtual or in-person)
4. Build rapport through consistent, respectful communication

**Cultural Notes:**
- 根回し (nemawashi) - Informal consensus building is essential
- Don't push for immediate meetings
- Focus on building 信頼 (trust) first
- Expect slow but steady progress

### Week 4: Meeting Requests
**Tasks:**
1. Send formal meeting request with specific agenda
2. Offer multiple time options with flexibility
3. Prepare for potential lengthy approval process
4. Have backup plan for introducing through partners/associations

## Phase 2: Engagement (Weeks 5-8)

### Week 5-6: Initial Meetings
**Meeting Preparation:**
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- Bring sufficient 名刺 (meishi - business cards) for all attendees
- Dress formally (suit and tie)
- Prepare to bow appropriately (お辞儀 - ojigi)
- Have Japanese materials ready, even if meeting is in English

**Meeting Structure:**
1. **Introduction (5-10 min):** Formal greetings and meishi exchange
2. **Relationship Building (10-15 min):** Light conversation, company background
3. **Main Presentation (20-30 min):** Value proposition, technical details
4. **Q&A (15-20 min):** Patient response to all questions
5. **Next Steps (5 min):** Gentle discussion of follow-up, no pressure

**Post-Meeting:**
- Send thank you email within 24 hours (in Japanese)
- Send thank you card/gift if culturally appropriate
- Follow up on any promised materials immediately
- Wait patiently for next steps (可能性は1-2週間)

### Week 7-8: Technical Demonstration
**Demo Preparation:**
- Ensure all technology works flawlessly (no tolerance for bugs)
- Have Japanese language UI prepared
- Demonstrate PlayStation integration prototype
- Show relevant metrics and data
- Prepare for detailed technical questions

**Demo Content:**
1. ScrollVerse platform overview
2. PlayStation integration possibilities
3. NFT creation and management for Sony Music artists
4. User engagement metrics and analytics
5. Security and privacy features (very important in Japan)

## Cultural Best Practices Throughout

### Communication Style
- **Always use 敬語 (keigo):** Honorific language in all written and verbal communication
- **Indirect communication:** Avoid direct refusals or confrontation
- **本音と建前 (honne/tatemae):** Understand difference between true feelings and public stance
- **阿吽の呼吸 (aun no kokyu):** Unspoken understanding - read between the lines

### Relationship Management
- **Long-term focus:** Emphasize sustained partnership, not short-term gains
- **Face-to-face valued:** In-person meetings highly preferred when possible
- **Group harmony (和 - wa):** Avoid actions that disrupt group consensus
- **Gift giving (お土産):** Small, thoughtful gifts appreciated at milestones

### Business Etiquette
- **Punctuality:** Be early, never late
- **Business cards:** Present and receive with both hands, read carefully
- **Seating:** Wait to be shown where to sit (hierarchy matters)
- **Drinking culture:** If invited to 飲み会 (nomikai), participate appropriately

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Rushing the process:** Japanese partnerships take time - expect 6-12 months
❌ **Being too direct:** Indirect communication is preferred
❌ **Ignoring hierarchy:** Always respect seniority and position
❌ **Skipping relationship building:** Business relationships are personal
❌ **Over-promising:** Under-promise and over-deliver is the Japanese way
❌ **Showing impatience:** Patience is a virtue highly valued
❌ **Ignoring details:** Japanese partners expect thoroughness
❌ **Negotiating aggressively:** Collaborative approach is expected
